News from times past

The Historical Herald is a newspaper with informative articles on all subjects, but they share one theme - they're set centuries ago! Read about Mongols, Ottomans, and many more as if they were your contemporaries. You can read it online for free or buy a physical copy. This project took a lot of time and effort (and one child, me, made everything - including the website), so if you're feeling generous, you can always donate a little :)!

The Historical Herald’s mission is to make history more fun. If you’re tired of hearing ‘in this year, they fought this battle, you’re in the right place. I feel like textbook history can be boring sometimes, and it’s a shame. History is such an exciting and interesting subject, and I think it should be presented like that. The Historical Herald is full of funny lines and little jokes to make you laugh. Needless to say, it should be accurate too. That’s why the articles feature real stories and how they happened to the best of our knowledge. I try to make sure that every detail is correct – I use place names how they were said at the time. Even the adverts on the sides of pages feature real historical concepts. Never heard of a hauberk or a gambeson? That’s ok – those historical terms are there to make The Historical Herald as immersive as possible. (By the way, they are types of medieval protective clothing). Also, among those facts (and jokes) are some little easter eggs that probably no one will notice. Kudos to you if you spot some of those!
